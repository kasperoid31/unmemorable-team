## 👁️ About the team

**Unmemorable Team** is a small, independent group of developers building **psychological horror mods** for Minecraft.

We started with one simple frustration: most "horror" content relies on the same three tricks — a loud noise, a monster lunging at the camera, and a screamer thumbnail. It works once. Then it's just annoying. The fear evaporates the moment you see the jumpscare coming.

We wanted the opposite. We wanted a kind of fear that doesn't *jump* at you — it **settles into you**. The feeling that the world you've been building in for hours has quietly stopped being yours. That something is off, and you can't quite prove it, and that uncertainty is worse than any monster.

So we don't make mods that scare you for one second. We make mods that stay with you **after you close the game** — the ones you think about while you're trying to fall asleep.

No jump scares. No bosses. No weapons. Just atmosphere, sound, and the slow, patient erosion of the thing you thought was safe.

## 🧭 Philosophy

These aren't marketing lines — they're the rules we actually hold ourselves to.

- 🚫 **No cheap jump scares.**
  Dread over shock. If a moment only works because it's loud and sudden, we cut it. Real horror is the ten quiet minutes *before* anything happens.

- 💾 **Everything is reversible. Your saves are safe.**
  We will mess with your *sense* of safety — never with your actual files. Whatever happens in the world can be undone, and nothing we do will corrupt or destroy your real save data.

- 📖 **Lore explains every mechanic.**
  Nothing happens "just because it's spooky." Every strange event has a reason inside the story. The deeper you dig, the more the pieces fit together — the horror and the lore are the same thing seen from two angles.

- 🎧 **Sound comes first.**
  Half of the fear lives in your headphones. We design audio before visuals, because a sound you can't explain is scarier than anything you can see.

- 🐢 **Slow is a feature.**
  We're not afraid of a quiet start. Tension that takes its time hits harder than tension handed to you in the first five minutes.
